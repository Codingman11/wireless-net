4. (Theory) Explain with your own words the basics of the following protocols. Whenever it is possible, compare their differences in terms of throughput, delay and implementation complexity. Use this slides to help.

  • Pure Aloha
  • Slotted Aloha
  • CSMA non-persistant
  • CSMA 1-persistant
  • CSMA p-persistant

Pure Aloha

In Pure Aloha, whenever the nodes has data, they sends it but it may cause collisions. For avoiding collision, the system uses ACK to detect these. It has lower throughput compared to Slotted Aloha due to increased chances of collisions.

Slotted Aloha

The nodes are only allowed to send during their slot which leads to less collision compared to Aloha. It has higher throughput compared to Pure Aloha

CSMA

This protocol, the nodes listens the channel before sending data. It has quite high throughput that minimize the collisions by listening the channel.

CSMA 1-presistent

In this protocol, nodes wait as long as the channel is busy, sending data only after the channel is idle. This has optimal throughput if the channel is lightly loaded.

CSMA p-presistent

This is a extension to CSMA where the nodes wait for a random backoff with a certain probability (p) or transmit with probability (1-p) The throughput of the protocol depends on the chosen probability
